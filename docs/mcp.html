Overview

A research assistant that knows the data

CHIP50 is a large-scale, nationally representative panel survey that has tracked how Americans use social media — and how that use connects to political attitudes and mental health — across 38+ waves since 2020. Each wave includes approximately 10,000 respondents, weighted to match the U.S. adult population.

This project gives you a way to query that data through conversation. Instead of writing SQL or running statistical software, you describe what you want to know in plain English. Claude selects the appropriate analysis tools, runs population-weighted queries against the CHIP50 BigQuery database, and returns tables, trend analyses, regression output, and prose summaries.

The system uses the Model Context Protocol (MCP), an open standard that lets AI assistants call external tools. The CHIP50 MCP server is deployed on Google Cloud Run and exposes 24 specialized analysis tools — from simple crosstabs to full OLS and logistic regression with survey weights, and publication-quality PDF report generation.

How it works
1
You ask a question in plain English
Type a research question inside Claude Desktop or Claude.ai — no special syntax required.
2
Claude selects the right tools
Claude automatically chooses from 24 specialized tools — crosstabs, trend lines, regressions, platform summaries, PDF report generation — based on your question.
3
The MCP server queries BigQuery
A cloud server runs population-weighted queries against the CHIP50 panel data with automatic privacy protections (cell suppression n < 30).
4
You get an answer ready to use
Results come back as formatted tables, trend charts, regression output, and plain-English summaries — ready for a paper, presentation, or further analysis.

What's in the dataset

CHIP50 covers 232 variables across platforms, demographics, political attitudes, media consumption, and mental health.

  • TikTok, YouTube, Snapchat

  • Pinterest, Messenger, Tumblr

  • Mastodon, Bluesky, Threads, 4chan
  • Usage, frequency, trust, posting

  • Age (8-category)
  • Gender
  • Race / Ethnicity (5-category)
  • Education level
  • Income (10-category)
  • Party ID (3- and 7-point)
  • Urban / suburban / rural type
  • All 50 states + DC
🧠
Mental Health
  • Full PHQ-9 scale (9 items)
  • Depressive symptom severity
  • Suicidal ideation item
  • Sleep, concentration, energy
  • Anhedonia, hopelessness
  • Clinical cutoff scoring
🗳️
Political Attitudes
  • Ideology (7-point scale)
  • Voting behavior
  • Institutional trust (6 items)
  • Trust in science, government
  • Trust in media & tech platforms
  • Political posting behavior
📅
Waves & Coverage
  • Waves 14–38+ (ongoing)
  • ~10,000 respondents per wave
  • Survey weights provided
  • Panel design (repeat measures)
  • Wave-specific date ranges
🔒
Privacy Protections
  • Aggregated data only — no records
  • Cell suppression (n < 30)
  • PHQ-9: aggregate outputs only
  • Sentinel values filtered (−99)
  • No personally identifiable info
  • OAuth authentication required
Sample Queries

What you can ask

From a quick one-liner to a full research paper — examples of what the system can handle, in order of complexity.

01
Suicidal Ideation by State Quick
Generate a choropleth map showing variation in suicidal ideation (PHQ-9 item 9) prevalence across all 50 states + DC.
generate_crosstab generate_marginals
Prompt
Create a choropleth map showing variation in suicidal ideation at the state level. Use the PHQ-9 item 9 data and display results as a percentage of respondents who reported any suicidal ideation (score ≥ 1).
What you get
A population-weighted state-level table of PHQ-9 item 9 prevalence (suppressed for states with n < 30), ready to be visualized as a choropleth map — or rendered directly using Claude's code generation. States with the highest and lowest rates are highlighted in a brief summary.
02
TikTok Adoption by Age and Party Quick
Cross-tabulate TikTok usage against age group and political party in the most recent wave.
generate_crosstab_batch
Prompt
Show me TikTok adoption rates broken down by age group and by political party in the most recent wave. Format as a table with percentages.
What you get
Two population-weighted tables showing what share of each age group and each partisan group uses TikTok, with sample sizes. Both crosstabs run in parallel.
03
Twitter/X Usage Trend Across All Waves Quick
Plot the trajectory of Twitter/X adoption including the period surrounding the Musk acquisition.
get_platform_trends
Prompt
Show me how Twitter/X usage has changed across all available survey waves. Plot the trend and note any sharp increases or decreases.
What you get
A wave-by-wave time series of Twitter/X adoption rates with exact field dates, with percentage-point changes between waves flagged for significant shifts.
04
Institutional Trust by Political Party Quick
Compare trust levels in science, government, social media companies, and specific platforms across partisan groups.
get_ordinal_crosstab generate_marginals_batch
Prompt
Compare trust in science, trust in social media companies, and trust in Facebook and Twitter broken down by political party. Use the most recent wave.
What you get
Mean trust scores (1–4 scale) for each institution by partisan group, in a comparison table with statistically meaningful differences highlighted.
01
Demographic Profiles of the Top 10 Platforms Over Time Moderate
For each of the 10 most-used platforms, describe the full demographic profile and show how it has shifted across waves — with comparisons to published third-party data.
get_platform_posting_summary generate_crosstab_batch get_platform_trends
Prompt
What are the demographic profiles of the top 10 social media platforms by usage, and how have those profiles changed over time? For each platform, show breakdowns by age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, and party ID, and track how these have shifted across survey waves. Where possible, note how the CHIP50 data aligns with or diverges from publicly available platform demographic data.
What you get
One section per platform: a demographic heatmap table for the most recent wave, a trend chart of overall adoption, a brief narrative profile, and a note on how patterns compare to published third-party research.
02
Platform Migration After the Musk Acquisition Moderate
Examine whether Twitter/X users migrated to alternative platforms after October 2022, and which demographic groups drove any shift.
get_platform_trends generate_crosstab_filtered run_logistic_regression
Prompt
Analyze platform migration away from Twitter/X following the Musk acquisition. Show trends in Twitter/X usage alongside adoption trends for Mastodon, Bluesky, and other alternatives. Break down which demographic and partisan groups drove any observed decline. Run a logistic regression predicting Twitter/X abandonment as a function of demographics and political affiliation.
What you get
A side-by-side trend analysis, a demographic breakdown of who left vs. who stayed, and a logistic regression table showing odds ratios for platform abandonment by demographic group — with a plain-English summary of key findings.
03
Political Sorting Across Platforms Over Time Moderate
Estimate the partisan composition of each platform's user base and examine how ideological clustering has changed across waves.
generate_crosstab_batch get_platform_trends get_ordinal_crosstab
Prompt
For each major social media platform, show the partisan composition of the user base (% Democrat, % Republican, % Independent) in the most recent wave. Then show how ideological balance has shifted across waves for the five most politically skewed platforms. Also show mean ideology scores (7-point scale) for users of each platform.
What you get
A ranked table of platforms by partisan skew, trend lines for the most polarized platforms, and a mean ideology score comparison across all platforms — illustrating which have become more ideologically sorted over time.
01
Research Paper: Social Media Use and Depression Complex
Generate a complete academic paper — theory-grounded hypotheses, research design, empirical tests with regression tables, and a discussion with references.
run_ols_regression run_logistic_regression get_platform_trends generate_crosstab_batch get_ordinal_distribution
Prompt
Write a research paper on social media use and depression using the CHIP50 data. Structure it in four sections: 1. Hypotheses — lay out theoretically grounded hypotheses about the relationship between social media use and depression, drawing on the literature (displacement theory, social comparison, passive vs. active use, platform-specific effects, demographic moderation). 2. Research Design — describe how to test each hypothesis using the available CHIP50 variables, specifying the outcome measure, key predictors, controls, and appropriate statistical approach. 3. Results — test each hypothesis with the data, producing regression tables with coefficients, standard errors, and significance levels, plus a brief paragraph per hypothesis on whether it is supported, partially supported, or rejected. 4. Discussion and Conclusion — summarize key findings, note limitations, and situate results in the broader literature. Provide a reference list.
What you get
A full draft research paper (~3,000–5,000 words) with a literature-grounded theory section, methods with variable operationalization, actual regression tables run against CHIP50 data (survey-weighted, proper reference categories), hypothesis-by-hypothesis results writeups, a discussion section addressing limitations, and an APA-format reference list.
02
Social Media Use and Political Polarization Complex
Investigate whether heavy social media use predicts more extreme political attitudes, and whether the effect varies by platform and demographic group.
run_ols_regression generate_crosstab_filtered get_ordinal_crosstab get_platform_trends
Prompt
Investigate whether social media use predicts more extreme ideological positions. Using ideology (7-point scale) as the outcome, run OLS regressions with platform usage frequency as predictors, controlling for demographics. Test whether the relationship differs by platform. Also run interaction models to test whether the effect of social media use on ideology is moderated by age, education, or party ID. Produce a full set of regression tables and a narrative summary.
What you get
A series of OLS models — baseline demographics, platform-specific, and interaction models — with tables showing platform-by-platform coefficients for ideology and a narrative discussing which platforms show the strongest associations and how those effects vary across groups.
03
The Geography of Social Media Use Complex
A comprehensive geographic analysis of platform adoption, usage intensity, and political posting across U.S. states and urban/rural typology.
generate_crosstab generate_crosstab_filtered run_ols_regression get_freq_trends
Prompt
Conduct a comprehensive geographic analysis of social media use. First, produce state-level adoption rates for the five largest platforms and identify regional patterns. Second, examine how urban/rural typology mediates platform choice. Third, analyze political posting behavior geographically — which states and urban/rural categories show the highest rates? Fourth, run a regression predicting frequency of political posting with state partisanship, urban type, age, and income as predictors. Generate choropleth-ready data tables for each major finding.
What you get
State-level data tables for each platform (choropleth-ready), an urban/rural platform preference analysis, a political posting geography table, and a regression model of political posting determinants — with a structured narrative connecting the geographic patterns.
Tools Reference

What Claude can call

Claude automatically selects the appropriate tools for your question. All queries return population-weighted estimates with cell suppression for privacy.

get_question_wording

Survey Question Wording

Returns the exact survey text for any CHIP50 variable — the question stem, response labels, and scale. Call this to confirm what a variable measures before citing it in a report.

get_wave_metadata

Wave Metadata

Per-wave respondent counts, field dates, and platform/question coverage flags. Essential for methodology sections and for checking which questions were fielded in a given wave.

generate_marginals_by_wave

Variable Distribution Across Waves

Full response distribution of one variable across all waves, optionally stratified by a demographic group. The primary tool for tracking how any attitude or behavior has shifted over time.

generate_crosstab_by_wave

Platform × Demographic Across Waves

Adoption rates for one platform broken down by a demographic group, tracked wave by wave. Use this to see whether a demographic gap is widening, narrowing, or stable over time.

summarize_pattern_by_wave

Trend Pattern Summary

Structured summary of multi-wave trends: for each demographic group, the direction and magnitude of change; for each pair of groups, whether their gap is stable, widening, narrowing, or reversed.

generate_crosstab_multi

Multi-Dimensional Crosstab

Variable broken down by the intersection of two or more demographic dimensions simultaneously — e.g. TikTok adoption by gender × party. Replaces running a regression just to get cell estimates.

get_ordinal_distribution_by_demographic

Full Ordinal Distribution by Group

Complete response distribution (% at each scale point) for an ordinal variable, broken down by demographic group. Unlike the crosstab mean, this shows you the full profile — e.g. % Strongly support vs. % Strongly oppose.

get_categorical_crosstab

Categorical Variable Crosstab

Weighted % distribution of a nominal categorical variable (e.g. primary news source, GLP-1 status) broken down by a demographic group. Handles non-ordinal, multi-category response items.

generate_pdf_report

PDF Report Generation

Assembles a publication-quality PDF with a title page, abstract, numbered tables, Pew-style charts, auto-generated methodology section, and a reproducibility appendix listing every tool call used. Returns a signed download URL.

get_report_status

Report Job Status

Polls the status of an async PDF report job and returns the download URL when it's ready. Use after calling generate_pdf_report for large reports that take a few seconds to build.

get_available_variables

Variable Discovery

Returns all available columns, their categories, and the wave range in the dataset. Claude calls this first to orient itself to the data structure.

generate_marginals

Single-Variable Distribution

Population-weighted distribution of one variable — e.g., what share of respondents use each platform, or how PHQ-9 scores are distributed.

generate_marginals_batch

Batch Distributions

Runs marginal distributions for multiple variables in parallel. Efficient for a snapshot of many platforms or survey items at once.

generate_crosstab

Platform × Demographic Crosstab

Adoption rates for one platform broken down by one demographic variable in a given wave, with weighted percentages and sample sizes.

generate_crosstab_filtered

Filtered Crosstab

Like generate_crosstab, but with additional demographic filters — e.g., TikTok usage by age, among women only.

generate_crosstab_batch

One Platform, Many Demographics

Runs crosstabs for one platform across multiple demographic dimensions simultaneously. Ideal for building a complete demographic profile.

get_platform_trends

Adoption Trends Over Time

Wave-by-wave adoption rates for a platform, optionally broken down by a demographic group, enabling time-series analysis.

get_freq_trends

Usage Frequency Trends

Tracks mean usage frequency (1–6 ordinal) for a platform across waves — capturing not just whether people use a platform but how intensely.

get_ordinal_distribution

Ordinal Variable Distribution

Full distribution of ordinal variables: usage frequency, trust scales (1–4), political ideology (1–7), and PHQ-9 item scores.

get_ordinal_crosstab

Mean Scores by Demographic

Mean ordinal scores by demographic group — e.g., mean PHQ-9 total by age, or mean platform trust by party identification.

get_platform_posting_summary

Full Platform Summary

All key metrics for one platform in a single call: adoption rate, usage frequency, trust score, and political posting behavior.

run_ols_regression

OLS Regression

Population-weighted OLS regression with user-specified outcome, predictors, and reference categories. Returns coefficients, standard errors, t-statistics, p-values, and R².

run_logistic_regression

Logistic Regression

Population-weighted logistic regression for binary outcomes — platform adoption, clinical depression cutoff, etc. Returns odds ratios and 95% confidence intervals.

introduce_mcp

Server Introduction

Plain-English overview of the MCP server, available tools, and the dataset structure. A useful starting point when exploring the system.
