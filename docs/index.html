Featured Report
REPORT · JULY 2026 · PLATFORMS · DEMOCRACY

Trust in Social Media Has Barely Moved — but Its Politics Flipped

Across nine survey waves from 2023 to 2026, Americans' trust in social media news stayed low and flat. What changed was partisan: Republican trust rose on nearly every platform, the Democratic edge narrowed, and on X it reversed outright.

Platforms Trust Partisanship Wave 38

Using 11 waves of CHIP50 panel data (Waves 28–38, mid-2023 to 2026), this report tracks trust in social media news across 20 platforms by party ID. Trust in X flipped from a 5-point Democratic lead to an 11-point Republican lead — a 16-point swing. On every mainstream platform the Republican-minus-Democrat gap moved in the Republican direction, while alternative platforms like Parler and Truth Social lost ground among their own users.

How It Works

Ask a question.
Get a publication-ready report.

The CHIP50 MCP server connects Claude AI directly to 38+ waves of national panel data on social media use, political attitudes, and mental health. Ask a research question in plain English — no SQL, no scripting required.

Claude selects from 24 specialized analysis tools, runs population-weighted queries against the CHIP50 BigQuery database, and delivers tables, trend analyses, regression output, and full PDF reports — ready for a paper, brief, or press release.

View MCP Documentation →

Connect Claude to the MCP
Add the CHIP50 connector in Claude's settings. Authenticate with Google OAuth. The server is already live — no installation, no API keys.
02
Ask your research question
Type any research question in plain English. Claude automatically selects the right combination of tools and executes them in sequence or parallel.

03
Receive publication-ready output
Population-weighted tables, trend charts, regression results with confidence intervals, and full PDF reports — with title pages, methodology sections, and reproducibility appendices.
04
Privacy-by-design, throughout
Cell suppression (n < 30) at the SQL layer. PHQ-9 returned as population-level aggregates only. No PII. OAuth authentication. All outputs are weighted, anonymized estimates.
